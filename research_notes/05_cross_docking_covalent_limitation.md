# Research Note 05 — Cross-Docking Reveals Limits of Non-Covalent Vina Scoring

**Date:** 2026-06-29
**Author:** Ethan Im

## Objective

Quantify mutant-selectivity of GA-generated candidates via cross-docking:
each candidate (WT-origin and T790M-origin) was docked against both the
wild-type and T790M receptor structures.

## Method

Full 2x2 cross-docking matrix: 10 candidates x 2 receptors = 20 Vina runs.
Repeated at exhaustiveness=8 and exhaustiveness=32 to confirm reproducibility.

## Results

| Ligand origin | Native receptor score | Cross receptor score | Selective? |
|---|---|---|---|
| WT candidates (avg) | -7.99 (on WT) | -7.90 (on T790M) | Weakly WT-selective |
| T790M candidates (avg) | -8.08 (on T790M) | -8.78 (on WT) | **Not T790M-selective** |

T790M-origin candidates bound MORE strongly to the wild-type receptor than to
their intended T790M target in every single case (5/5), at both
exhaustiveness=8 and exhaustiveness=32. This pattern was fully reproducible,
ruling out sampling noise as an explanation.

## Interpretation

This is an honest negative result for the selectivity hypothesis as measured
by standard non-covalent Vina scoring. The most plausible explanation is
mechanistic: T790M candidates generated by the GA prominently feature an
acrylamide warhead (C=CC(=O)N-), whose primary mechanism of action in real
3rd-generation EGFR-TKIs (e.g. osimertinib) is irreversible COVALENT bond
formation with Cys797 near the T790M pocket. Standard AutoDock Vina computes
only non-covalent binding energy and cannot capture this covalent contribution.
As a result, Vina scoring likely underestimates the true mutant-selective
potency of these candidates, while overweighting their non-covalent fit to
the (larger, more permissive) wild-type pocket.

This does not invalidate the GA's candidate generation -- the model learned
a real, mechanistically grounded structural motif (Research Note 02) -- but
it does reveal a validation gap: non-covalent docking is an inappropriate
tool for evaluating covalent-mechanism candidates, and this limitation should
be stated explicitly rather than obscured.

## Implications for the Paper

The original framing ("T790M candidates show preferential binding to the
mutant receptor") based on native-only docking (Research Note 04) is not
supported by the full cross-docking analysis and must be corrected.
The corrected, more accurate claim is: GA-generated T790M candidates contain
a mechanistically plausible covalent warhead, but their mutant-selectivity
cannot be confirmed (and is in fact contradicted) by non-covalent docking
scores alone -- highlighting the need for covalent docking methods
(e.g. CovDock, AutoDock-GPU covalent mode) in future validation.
