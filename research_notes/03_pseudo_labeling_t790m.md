# Research Note 03 — Pseudo-Labeling Resolves T790M Data Scarcity

**Date:** 2026-06-(today)
**Author:** Ethan Im

## Research Question

Following Research Note 01 (T790M model underperforms wild-type due
to ~9x less training data) and the PI1M pseudo-labeling strategy used
successfully in Polyinverse: can pseudo-labeling the wild-type EGFR
compound pool (which is a superset of the T790M-tested compounds)
recover the lost performance of the T790M affinity model?

## Method

- Source of pseudo-labels: 12,348 wild-type EGFR compounds with no
  recorded T790M measurement in ChEMBL, labeled using the existing
  T790M model (Research Note 01, R=0.40) as a weak teacher.
- Combined with the 1,552 real T790M training samples.
- Weighted MSE loss: real samples weight = 1.0, pseudo-labeled
  samples weight = 0.3, so real data dominates gradient updates.
- Same AttentiveFP architecture, same train/val/test split as before.

## Results (Test Set)

| Model                      | Train n | RMSE  | R     | R²    |
|-----------------------------|---------|-------|-------|-------|
| Wild-type EGFR              | 14,098  | 0.890 | 0.736 | 0.534 |
| T790M (real only)           | 1,552   | 0.920 | 0.400 | 0.155 |
| T790M (real + pseudo, w=0.3)| 13,900  | 0.717 | 0.698 | 0.487 |

## Observations

1. Pseudo-labeling closed most of the gap to wild-type performance:
   R improved from 0.40 to 0.70 (+0.30), R² from 0.155 to 0.487
   (+0.33). RMSE on the T790M test set is even lower than wild-type's
   own RMSE, though this is partly because the pseudo-label source
   pool overlaps with wild-type's distribution.
2. This confirms the central hypothesis from Note 01: the T790M
   model's earlier weakness was substantially a data-scarcity problem,
   not an inherent limit of the architecture or chemical space.
3. Caveat: pseudo-labels were generated by the *weak* T790M teacher
   model (R=0.40), so there is a circularity risk — the student may
   partly be learning to imitate the teacher's biases rather than
   true T790M activity. The fact that performance still improved
   substantially suggests the wild-type structural signal carried
   real, transferable information, but this should be stated as a
   limitation, not overclaimed as ground-truth-level improvement.
4. Practically, this validates reusing the Polyinverse PI1M strategy
   across a structurally different domain (drug discovery vs.
   polymers), which strengthens the "consistent inverse-design
   methodology across materials and drug discovery" narrative for
   the portfolio.
